Battle of Udaipur

Introduction:

  • After the death of Maharana Raj Singh I in 1680, Aurangzeb attempted to subjugate Mewar completely.
  • He sent a large force to capture Udaipur.
  • The Mewari ruler Maharana Jai Singh skillfully employed guerrilla tactics, using the rugged Aravalli terrain to his advantage.
  • The Mughals failed to capture Udaipur, suffering heavy losses.

Battle:

Udaipur's rulers sometimes found themselves entangled between the British East India Company and Maratha powers (like the Holkars and Scindias). Though not a direct “Battle of Udaipur,” conflicts in the region influenced its political alignment. Eventually, Udaipur came under British protection in 1818 via a subsidiary alliance.
